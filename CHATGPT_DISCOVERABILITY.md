# How ChatGPT Will Find Your "Buzzed Out" Game

## üîç How ChatGPT Searches for Information

ChatGPT uses several methods to find and reference websites:

### 1. **Web Search Integration**
- ChatGPT has access to web search (when enabled)
- It uses search engines (primarily Bing/Google) to find current information
- When you ask "Buzzed out online game", it searches the web and returns relevant results

### 2. **Training Data**
- ChatGPT's training data includes information up to its knowledge cutoff date
- If your site was indexed before the cutoff, it might be in the training data
- **However**, for new sites, web search is the primary method

### 3. **Real-time Web Browsing**
- Some ChatGPT versions can browse the web in real-time
- It can visit URLs and read content directly
- This is the most reliable way for new sites to be discovered

## üéØ What Needs to Happen for ChatGPT to Find Your Game

### Step 1: Your Site Must Be Publicly Accessible ‚úÖ
- Deploy your game to a public URL (not localhost)
- Make sure it's accessible without login/authentication
- Example: `https://buzzedout-game.com` or `https://your-domain.com`

### Step 2: Get Indexed by Search Engines
ChatGPT primarily uses search engines, so your site needs to be indexed:

#### Google Indexing:
1. **Submit to Google Search Console**
   - Go to [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console)
   - Add your property (your website URL)
   - Verify ownership
   - Submit your sitemap: `https://your-domain.com/sitemap.xml`
   - Request indexing for your homepage

2. **Wait for Indexing**
   - Google typically indexes sites within a few days to weeks
   - You can check if you're indexed by searching: `site:your-domain.com`

#### Bing Indexing:
1. **Submit to Bing Webmaster Tools**
   - Go to [Bing Webmaster Tools](https://www.bing.com/webmasters)
   - Add your site
   - Submit your sitemap

### Step 3: Have Clear, Descriptive Content
ChatGPT needs to understand what your site is about:

#### On Your Homepage, Include:
- **Clear title**: "Buzzed Out - Pass the Card"
- **Description**: What the game is, how to play
- **Keywords**: "Buzzed Out", "online game", "multiplayer party game"
- **Plain text content** (not just in meta tags)

Example of good homepage content:
```html
<h1>Buzzed Out - Pass the Card</h1>
<p>Play Buzzed Out, a free online multiplayer party game! 
Two teams compete by guessing phrases and passing cards. 
The team holding the card when the hidden timer ends loses.</p>
```

### Step 4: Build Backlinks and Mentions
- Get other websites to link to your game
- Share on social media
- Post on Reddit, game forums, etc.
- Submit to game directories (itch.io, Game Jolt, etc.)
- More mentions = higher chance ChatGPT will find it
